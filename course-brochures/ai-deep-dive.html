About This Course
What is AI & ML Engineering — 12 Weeks?

This is the program for people who are done being on the outside of AI looking in. Over 12 weeks, you'll go from the mathematical foundations of machine learning all the way to training neural networks, fine-tuning large language models, and deploying production-ready AI systems. Code-first throughout — every concept is implemented, not just explained.


The program is structured in four phases: ML Foundations (statistics, regression, evaluation), Core Algorithms (trees, SVMs, clustering, dimensionality reduction), Deep Learning (neural networks from scratch, PyTorch, CNNs, RNNs, transfer learning), and Applied AI & Capstone (LLM fine-tuning with LoRA, model deployment, and your capstone project). Each phase builds on the last — by Week 12 you'll have the context to understand why every choice along the way was made.


The capstone project is the centrepiece of Month 3. You'll design, build, and deploy a real AI product — something you would genuinely use or could show to an employer. Weekly assignments with model answers, code reviews, and a structured interview prep module complete the picture.

Curriculum
12 Weeks, Four Phases
Weeks 1–3
ML Foundations
  • Statistics & probability for ML — distributions, expectations, Bayes' theorem, MLE; the statistical intuition that underlies every ML algorithm
  • Linear & logistic regression from scratch — implementing both algorithms in NumPy; understanding the loss function, gradient, and update rule
  • Feature engineering & selection — how to transform raw data into useful features; normalisation, encoding, and feature importance
  • Model evaluation — accuracy, precision, recall, F1, ROC-AUC; overfitting vs. underfitting; cross-validation and the bias-variance tradeoff
Weeks 4–6
Core Algorithms
  • Decision trees & Random Forests — how tree-based models split data, why ensembles work, and when to reach for gradient boosting
  • SVMs & kernel methods — support vector machines, the kernel trick, and maximum margin classification explained geometrically
  • Clustering — K-Means, DBSCAN — unsupervised learning; grouping unlabelled data and evaluating cluster quality
  • Dimensionality reduction — PCA, t-SNE — reducing high-dimensional data for visualisation, compression, and improved model performance
Weeks 7–9
Deep Learning
  • Neural networks from scratch (NumPy) — implementing forward pass, loss calculation, backpropagation, and gradient descent entirely in NumPy
  • PyTorch fundamentals — tensors, autograd, nn.Module, training loops; the framework used by the majority of production ML teams
  • CNNs for vision, RNNs/LSTMs for sequences — architectures for structured data types; training on image and sequence datasets
  • Transfer learning & fine-tuning — using pre-trained models as starting points; when and how to fine-tune vs. train from scratch
Weeks 10–12
Applied AI & Capstone
  • LLM APIs & fine-tuning (LoRA) — working with large language model APIs and fine-tuning open-source models on custom datasets using LoRA efficiently
  • Model deployment — wrapping models in FastAPI, containerising with Docker, and deploying to cloud infrastructure
  • Capstone project — build your own ML product from data collection through training, evaluation, API, and deployment; mentored sprint
  • Portfolio, GitHub, interview prep — presenting your technical work effectively; common ML interview questions and how to approach them

Will I need a GPU for this program?
No — we use Google Colab (free GPU) for deep learning exercises. A regular laptop with stable internet is sufficient for the entire program.
How rigorous is the maths?
We introduce maths where it deepens understanding, but always lead with intuition first. You need algebra-level comfort; calculus and linear algebra are taught as needed within the program.
Is this program suitable for career switchers?
Yes — many alumni have used the capstone project and certificate to transition into ML engineering or data science roles. The interview prep module specifically addresses this.
