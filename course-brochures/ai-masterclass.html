About This Course
What is AI Demystified in 3 Hours?

The word "AI" is used so broadly and so loosely that most people — including many who work in tech — don't actually understand what it means or how it works. This session fixes that. In three hours, you'll develop a genuine, accurate understanding of how machine learning works, how neural networks learn, and specifically how the large language models (LLMs) like ChatGPT and Claude actually function under the hood.


Critically, this session doesn't require a maths degree or programming background. Everything is taught through visual intuition and live demonstrations first. The session is structured around the most common questions people have: What is a model? What does "training" actually mean? How does a neural network learn? Why do LLMs sometimes make things up? You'll leave with real answers to all of these.


The third hour moves from understanding to doing — you'll see a live demonstration of calling OpenAI and Anthropic APIs, understand how prompt engineering works, and leave knowing exactly how to start building with AI if you choose to.

Curriculum
What You'll Cover, Hour by Hour
Hour 1
What Machines Actually Learn
  • What ML really is — the crucial distinction between traditional programming (rules → output) and machine learning (data → rules); why this changed everything
  • Training, features & labels explained visually — how a model learns from examples; what training data actually looks like and what the training process does
  • Linear models & decision boundaries — the simplest ML model explained geometrically; classification, regression, and why simple models still matter
  • Why deep learning suddenly worked — the historical context: what changed around 2012 that made neural networks practical, and why it matters
Hour 2
Neural Networks & LLMs
  • How a neural network learns — forward pass (making a prediction) and backward pass (learning from mistakes) explained through visual analogy, no calculus required
  • Backpropagation explained intuitively — the algorithm behind every neural network; what it's doing conceptually and why it works
  • What a transformer actually does — the architecture behind GPT, Claude, and every modern LLM; attention mechanisms explained in plain English
  • How GPT-4 and Claude work under the hood — next-token prediction, context windows, temperature, and what it means for how you use these tools
Hour 3
AI in Practice
  • Live: building with AI APIs — a real-time demo of calling OpenAI and Anthropic APIs; you'll see exactly how developers build AI-powered applications
  • Prompt engineering principles — what actually works and why: zero-shot vs. few-shot prompting, chain-of-thought, system prompts — with live examples
  • Where AI fails — hallucinations, bias, knowledge cutoffs, reasoning limits; understanding these is essential for using AI responsibly and effectively
  • Roadmap: how to go deeper — an honest map of the AI learning landscape; which path makes sense based on your goals and background
