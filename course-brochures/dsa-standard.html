About This Course
What is DSA for Interviews — 6 Weeks?

The dirty secret of technical interview preparation is that most candidates approach it the wrong way. They grind hundreds of LeetCode problems hoping patterns emerge through repetition — and then freeze in an interview when they see a problem that looks slightly different from what they practiced. This course teaches the opposite approach: pattern recognition first.


Over six weeks, you'll systematically learn the 15+ algorithmic patterns that cover the vast majority of problems asked in technical screens at product companies. Two pointers, sliding window, BFS/DFS, dynamic programming, backtracking — you'll know not just how to implement each pattern but how to recognise which one to reach for from the first few lines of a problem statement.


The course includes 100 curated problems, pattern cheat sheets, and three full mock interviews with detailed written feedback — the same structure used by candidates who successfully clear screens at top product companies. By Week 6 you'll be solving medium LeetCode problems consistently and approaching interviews with a structured, confident methodology.

Curriculum
Six Weeks, Four Modules, 15+ Patterns
Weeks 1–2
Arrays, Strings & Hashing
  • Two pointers — the pattern for problems involving pairs, sorted arrays, and palindromes; left/right pointer and slow/fast pointer variants; when to recognise it
  • Sliding window — fixed and variable-size windows for substring, subarray, and sequence problems; the template that solves an entire category of O(n) problems elegantly
  • Hash maps & hash sets — O(1) lookup as a problem-solving tool; frequency counting, anagram detection, two-sum and its variants; the pattern behind dozens of common problems
  • Prefix sums & difference arrays — precomputing cumulative sums to answer range queries in O(1); the pattern behind subarray sum problems and range update operations
Week 3
Trees & Graphs
  • Binary trees — traversals & properties — inorder, preorder, postorder, and level-order traversals both recursively and iteratively; height, diameter, LCA, and path problems
  • Binary search trees — BST properties, validation, insertion, deletion, and the range of interview problems that exploit BST ordering
  • DFS & BFS — depth-first and breadth-first search as universal graph traversal patterns; when to use each; iterative DFS with a stack vs. recursive DFS
  • Graph representations & common patterns — adjacency list vs. matrix, connected components, cycle detection, topological sort, and union-find for disjoint sets
Week 4
Recursion & Dynamic Programming
  • Recursion patterns & call stack — thinking recursively; the recursive leap of faith; identifying base cases and recursive cases; tracing through the call stack
  • Memoization (top-down DP) — converting brute-force recursion to efficient DP by caching subproblem results; the pattern that makes exponential problems polynomial
  • Bottom-up DP with tables — building DP tables iteratively; space optimisation; transitioning from memoization to tabulation for interview clarity
  • Classic DP problems — knapsack (0/1 and unbounded), longest common subsequence, coin change, edit distance, and the decision framework for identifying DP problems
Weeks 5–6
Advanced Topics & Mock Interviews
  • Heaps & priority queues — min-heap, max-heap, and the k-th element pattern; top-k problems, merge k sorted lists, and the heap as a running minimum/maximum structure
  • Tries — prefix tree structure, word search, autocomplete, and problems requiring fast prefix lookups
  • Greedy algorithms — the greedy choice property; recognising when locally optimal choices lead to globally optimal solutions; interval scheduling and activity selection
  • Backtracking — the exhaustive search pattern for permutations, combinations, and constraint satisfaction; pruning techniques that make it practical
  • 3 full mock interviews with written feedback — conducted under real interview conditions; detailed written feedback on your problem-solving approach, communication, and code quality

The course teaches in Python — it's the most interview-friendly language for clarity. You can follow along in any language (Java, C++, JavaScript) but code examples and templates will be in Python.
How are the mock interviews scheduled?
Mock interviews are scheduled in Weeks 4, 5, and 6 based on your availability. They're conducted 1-on-1 over video call and last 45–60 minutes each.

This course prepares you for the coding round at most product companies. Top-tier FAANG interviews (Google L5+, Meta E5+) also require system design preparation and harder algorithm problems. We recommend this as the DSA foundation, with additional hard problem practice for top-tier targets.
