About This Course
What is Calculus in 3 Hours?

Most people who were taught calculus in school or college learned the rules — the power rule, the chain rule, integration by substitution — without ever understanding what they're really doing geometrically. This session reverses that. We start with the visual intuition: what a derivative really is (the slope of a curve at a point), what an integral really is (accumulated area), and why the Fundamental Theorem connects them. The formulas follow naturally once the picture is clear.


This is a focused three-hour session covering the two big ideas in calculus — differentiation and integration — along with their most important applications. It's designed for people who want to genuinely understand calculus, whether for competitive exams, university courses, or because they're learning machine learning and keep hitting gradient descent without knowing what a derivative actually is.


By the end you'll be able to differentiate any standard function, evaluate definite and indefinite integrals, and understand exactly how calculus powers gradient descent in machine learning. A 30-problem practice set with full solutions is included to reinforce everything covered.

Curriculum
What You'll Cover, Hour by Hour
Hour 1
Derivatives
  • What a derivative really means — the slope idea; instantaneous rate of change visualised geometrically as the limit of a secant line; why this is the central idea of differential calculus
  • Differentiation rules & shortcuts — power rule, sum rule, constant multiple rule derived from first principles, then applied fluently across polynomial, exponential, and trigonometric functions
  • Chain rule, product rule — explained visually — not just "here's the formula" but a geometric/intuitive explanation of why these rules are necessary and what they're capturing
  • Applications: rates of change & tangents — finding the slope of a curve at a point, velocity and acceleration, and the general framework for applying derivatives to real problems
Hour 2
Integrals
  • Integration as area accumulation — the visual definition of a definite integral as the area under a curve; Riemann sums explained; why this is a natural and useful idea
  • Antiderivatives & the reverse process — indefinite integration as the operation that reverses differentiation; building intuition for "which function has this derivative?"
  • Definite integrals & the Fundamental Theorem — the remarkable connection between derivatives and integrals; why the Fundamental Theorem is the most important result in calculus
  • Substitution method — the most important integration technique; how it relates to the chain rule in reverse; worked examples with a range of functions
Hour 3
Applications & ML Connections
  • Optimisation problems — maxima & minima — using derivatives to find where functions reach their maximum and minimum values; the critical point condition and second derivative test
  • Related rates — how derivatives let us relate the rates of change of connected quantities; a classic application area for physics and engineering
  • Where calculus appears in ML (gradient descent) — the direct line from derivatives to gradient descent; why the derivative of a loss function with respect to a parameter tells you how to update that parameter
  • Problem-solving session — working through selected problems from the included problem set, covering the problem-solving strategies that appear in exams and real applications
